DUTIES

A Wastewater Treatment Laboratory Manager plans, organizes and directs through subordinate supervisors the activities of a major unit or a division responsible for providing laboratory and/or scientific support services to the Bureau of Sanitation and General Services Department; prepares the budget for assigned group; directs and reviews a group of professional and technical laboratory staff responsible for providing chemical, biological, radiological, physical, and ecological data and assessments on wastewater treatment processes and environmental site investigation and assessment; provides characterization of air emissions and of wastewater and storm discharges, and impact of these discharges on receiving waters; provides control and research data on solid waste and air emissions; directs and conducts more difficult biological, chemical or physical assessments of monitoring data; applies sound supervisory principles and techniques in building and maintaining an effective work force; and fulfills equal employment opportunity responsibilities.

REQUIREMENTS/MINIMUM QUALIFICATIONS

1. Four years of full-time paid professional experience in a class at the level of Water Microbiologist, Chemist, or Water Biologist (pay grade I or II), performing a variety of chemical or biological laboratory or field tests of wastewater, marine water and sediments, storm water runoff, industrial waste, or air, in work related to wastewater treatment or storm water discharges; or
2. Two years of full-time paid professional experience with the City of Los Angeles in a class at the level of Senior Chemist or in a position at the level of Water Biologist III which provides experience supervising laboratory work related to wastewater treatment.

PROCESS NOTES

1. Applicants who lack six months or less of the required experience may file for this examination. However, they cannot be appointed until the full experience requirement is met.
2. For qualifying work experience gained outside of the City of Los Angeles, the term "professional experience" applies to positions that required possession of a degree from a recognized four-year college or university in order to obtain that position. Therefore, to be considered "professional," non-City qualifying experience must be gained in positions after obtaining a four-year degree.
3. Some positions may require a valid California driver's license. Candidates may not be eligible for appointment to these positions if their record within the last 36 months reflects three or more moving violations and/or at-fault accidents, or convictions of major moving violations (such as DUI).
4. Upon appointment, a Wastewater Treatment Laboratory Manager may be required to furnish an automobile, properly insured, for use in City service. Mileage will be paid in accordance with established rates.

           Interview  . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . .100%

The examination will consist entirely of an evaluation of professional and personal qualifications by interview. In the interview, emphasis will be placed on the candidate's background and experience as they have provided the knowledge of: the principles of chemistry, bacteriology, water biology, and biology; wastewater conveyance, treatment, and disposal; scientific method sufficient to successfully analyze, interpret, and report results of routine and special laboratory tests and projects; laboratory procedure, techniques, and equipment relating to wastewater and industrial waste analysis; sources of information fields related to water and wastewater laboratory testing; State, Federal, and local laws and regulations relating to environmental protection and testing; pertinent City and Personnel Department rules, policies, and procedures, including Equal Employment Opportunity; safety rules, policies, and procedures related to laboratory work including the provisions of CAL/OSHA; fundamental principle and practices of supervision; and the ability to initiate and complete research projects; plan, layout, direct, and coordinate activities of technical laboratory staff; develop and maintain technical and safety training programs; prepare reports, correspondence, research findings, and other documents in a clear, concise, and effective manner; deal tactfully and effectively with others; communicate in a clear and effective manner; and other necessary knowledge, skills, and abilities.

Prior to the interview, applicants will be required to prepare some written material related to the duties and responsibilities of a Wastewater Treatment Laboratory Manager. This essay material will not be separately scored, but will be presented to the interview board for discussion with the candidate and for consideration in the overall evaluation of the candidate. Please note that applicants must complete the advisory essay and meet minimum qualifications as stated on this bulletin in order to be considered further in the examination process.

The advisory essay will be administered on-line. Applicants invited to participate in the examination will receive an e-mail from the City of Los Angeles outlining the specific steps needed to complete the on-line advisory essay. Applicants will be required to complete the on-line advisory essay between MONDAY, DECEMBER 3, 2018 and MONDAY, DECEMBER 10, 2018. Additional instructions will be sent via e-mail. Applicants who fail to complete the advisory essay as instructed may be disqualified.

Candidates will be notified later by e-mail of the date, time and location of the interview, which will be held in Los Angeles. It is anticipated that the interviews will begin during the period of JANUARY 14, 2019 to JANUARY 25, 2019.
